What are millipedes?

Millipedes are arthropods belonging to the class Diplopoda. They are characterized by having two pairs of jointed legs on most body segments. With over 12,000 species worldwide, they are one of the most diverse groups of organisms on Earth.

How many legs do millipedes have?

The number of legs millipedes have can vary significantly, ranging from 30 to over 750 legs depending on the species. Despite their name meaning "thousand feet," no millipede species actually has 1,000 legs - the record holder is Illacme plenipes with up to 750 legs.

What do millipedes eat?

Millipedes are detritivores, meaning they primarily feed on decomposing plant material, dead leaves, and other organic matter. Some may also consume fungi and other plant-based materials. They play a crucial role in breaking down leaf litter and recycling nutrients in forest ecosystems.

Where do millipedes live?

Millipedes are typically found in moist environments such as forest floors, leaf litter, under rocks, and decaying logs. They prefer dark, humid conditions. In Nepal, they can be found from the lowland Terai forests up to high Himalayan regions.

Are millipedes harmful to humans?

Millipedes are generally harmless to humans. They do not bite or sting, but some species can release a defensive chemical that may cause mild skin irritation or allergic reactions in some people. It's always best to wash your hands after handling them.

How do millipedes defend themselves?

Millipedes can curl into a tight coil to protect their soft underbelly. Many species also secrete toxic or foul-smelling chemicals (including hydrogen cyanide) from their bodies to deter predators. This is why they sometimes smell bad when handled.

How do millipedes reproduce?

Millipedes reproduce by laying eggs. Males transfer sperm to females using specialized limbs called gonopods. Females lay eggs in the soil or other concealed, moist environments. Some species even build nests and guard their eggs until they hatch.

Why do I find millipedes in my house?

Millipedes may enter homes seeking moisture and shelter, especially during dry weather or heavy rain. They are usually found in basements, bathrooms, and other damp areas. Sealing cracks and reducing moisture around your home can help prevent them.

Do millipedes play a role in the ecosystem?

Yes, millipedes play a crucial role in the ecosystem by breaking down decaying plant material and recycling nutrients back into the soil, aiding in soil formation and fertility. They are essential decomposers in forest ecosystems and help create rich topsoil.

Can millipedes be kept as pets?

Yes, some species of millipedes are kept as pets. They require a habitat with high humidity, appropriate substrate (like coconut fiber or leaf litter), and a diet of decaying plant matter, fruits, and vegetables. Giant African millipedes are popular pet species.
